Tribute to Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, which is one of the two fundamental ideas in the field of physics along with quantum mechanics.

He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect and made significant contributions to the development of quantum mechanics.

Einstein is best known for his equation E=mcÂ², which demonstrates the equivalence of mass and energy, and for his groundbreaking work on the general theory of relativity.

He was not only a brilliant scientist but also an outspoken advocate for peace, civil rights, and scientific freedom.
